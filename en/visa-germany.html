Visa Options

Important Visa Types for Germany

🎯 Chancenkarte

Points-based immigration system for skilled workers. Minimum 6 points required. Valid for 12 months for job search.

Processing time: 2-4 weeks

💼 EU Blue Card

For highly qualified professionals with employment contract. Salary minimum €45,000 gross per year. Valid for 4 years.

Processing time: 2-4 weeks

🔧 Skilled Worker Visa

For skilled workers with recognized qualifications and employment contract. No points requirement. Valid for 4 years.

Processing time: 2-4 weeks

🎓 Student Visa

For students at German universities. University admission letter required. Valid for duration of studies.

Processing time: 4-8 weeks

🏫 Apprenticeship Visa

For apprentices in recognized vocational professions. Apprenticeship contract required. Valid for apprenticeship duration.

Processing time: 2-4 weeks

🌍 Visitor Visa

For tourists and visitors. Valid for 90 days in 180 days. No work rights.

Processing time: 1-2 weeks

Requirements

Basic Requirements for a Visa

Regardless of the visa type, there are basic requirements that all applicants must meet.

📄 Passport

A valid passport is required. It must remain valid throughout your stay in Germany.

🎓 Qualification

Depending on the visa type, a recognized degree, vocational training or university admission is required.

🗣️ Language Skills

German B1 or higher is required for most visas. English may be sufficient in some cases.

💰 Financial Means

Proof of ability to support yourself. Minimum €934 per month for students.

🏥 Health Insurance

Valid health insurance is required. German insurance must be obtained before arrival.

Process

Step-by-Step Visa Process

1

Prepare Documents

Gather all required documents: passport, qualification certificates, language certificates, financial proof, health insurance.

2

Book Appointment at Embassy

Contact the German embassy or consulate in your country. Schedule an appointment for visa application.

3

Submit Application

Complete the application form and submit all required documents. Pay the visa fee.

4

Receive Decision

Wait for the embassy's decision. If approved, you will receive your passport with the visa.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions About Visa for Germany

Which visa is best for me?

It depends on your situation. The Chancenkarte is ideal for skilled workers without an employment contract. The Blue Card is better for highly qualified professionals with an employment contract.

How long does visa processing take?

Most work visas are processed in 2-4 weeks. Student visas can take 4-8 weeks. It is recommended to apply early.

Can I bring my family to Germany?

Yes, spouses and children can follow with a family reunification visa. You need proof of family relationship.

Can I extend my visa?

Yes, most visas can be extended if you continue to meet the requirements. Apply for extension at least 6 weeks before expiration.
