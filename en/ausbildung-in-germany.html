German Vocational System

What is Vocational Training in Germany?

The German vocational training system is recognized worldwide for its quality and practical approach. Vocational training (Ausbildung) is a structured professional qualification that combines theory and practice.

Dual System

Combination of vocational school and practical work in a company.

Paid Training

Apprentices receive a monthly salary throughout their training.

Duration: 2-3 Years

Most training programs last 2-3 years, depending on the profession.

Career Opportunities

After training, many options exist for further education and advancement.

Financial Security

Salary During Vocational Training

Unlike many other countries, apprentices in Germany are paid. The salary varies depending on the industry and year of training.

Year of Training Average Salary
1st Year €800 - €1,000/month
2nd Year €900 - €1,150/month
3rd Year €1,000 - €1,300/month

Note: These are average salaries. Actual salaries may vary depending on the company, region, and industry.

Prerequisites

Requirements for Vocational Training in Germany

📚 School Diploma

A school diploma (secondary school or higher) is required. Foreign diplomas must be recognized.

🗣️ German Language

German B1 is ideal for most training programs. B2 is recommended for more complex professions.

💪 Motivation

Commitment, willingness to learn, and reliability are important for success in training.

📋 Visa

International applicants need a vocational training visa. The Chancenkarte can also be used for training.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQs About Vocational Training in Germany

Can I do vocational training in Germany as a foreigner?

Yes, international applicants can complete vocational training in Germany. You need a vocational training visa and must meet language requirements.

How long does vocational training take?

Most training programs last 2-3 years, depending on the profession and industry.

Can I work in Germany after completing training?

Yes, after successfully completing your training, you can work as a skilled professional. You can apply for a skilled worker visa.

What language do I need for vocational training?

German B1 is the minimum. B2 is recommended for most training programs to follow lessons and practical work effectively.
